Plant specialty lipid diversity and biosynthesis

The plant cuticle

Plants have large surface areas to maximize their photosynthetic capacity but in doing so risk getting dried out by the sun. To hinder water loss, plants seal their above ground surfaces with a lipidic coating called the cuticle. Cuin, a polyester matrix, serves as a molecular scaffold within and on top of which cuticular waxes, a mixture of very-long-chain lipids, accumulate to seal the surface (Figure 1). Both these classes of phytochemicals are essential to plant life on dry land and are crucial areas of research as our planet becomes warmer. Fundamental advances in our understanding of the cuticle will inform agricultural practices and enable better crops.
