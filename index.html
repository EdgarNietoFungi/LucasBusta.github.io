Lucas Busta

Analytical and Organic Chemistry, Biochemistry and Molecular Biology

I am interested in the structure, biosynthesis, and properties of phytochemicals; chemical compounds present in plants. These remarkable substances exhibit incredible structural and functional diversity and play important roles in the everyday lives of both plants and humans. To learn more about phytochemicals and why they are so fascinating please visit my phytochemistry blog.

As a graduate student in the lab of Prof. Reinhard Jetter in the The University of British Columbia's department of Chemistry, I study the chemical compounds that cover plant surfaces.

In 2017 I will be a post-doctoral researcher in the laboratory of Edgar Cahoon in the Center for Plant Science Innovation at the University of Nebraska - Lincoln. There I will be studying the biosynthesis of specialty plant lipids in crop species.
